![A 3ds Gaming Hanheld](/images/blog/3ds.jpg)

This has been something I´ve been wanting to for a long time, the idea of picking up an official game console than modify it beyond its original capacity and just do whatever I want with it.

And for a while I was also afraid to do it since I lacked any experience in the area, plus my 3DS stopped working, which made me think i would never get to hack it.

But the news of Nintendo officially closing the Eshop gave me the motivation to try again, because let' s face it, how else am I able to easily play some of these digital exclusive games?

## Preparation

![my red pokemon 3DS model](/images/2023/3dsredpokemon.jpeg#center)

So the first thing I did was trying to get my 3DS working again, I did some research, and turns out it might be something to do with my battery. Luckily there are plenty places online that will sell replaceable batteries, and it I got it after two weeks.

It also depends where you live, I ordered mine at the Fnac store.

So that´s step 1, getting a workable 3DS. Now for the hacking part.

As it turns out, thanks to years of effort in the homebrew scene, anyone and I mean anyone can easily mod it. The go-to guide is the [**3DS Hacks guide**](https://3ds.hacks.guide/), this is the definitive one to follow and you dont need anything else.

I was advised against following youtube guides, since for one some of them might be outdated, and also by following through this site alone, you can avoid bricking.

I followed the instructions by the letter. First I checked my SD card for errors and if its on the right format.

I also mentioned the model and system version. Mine is an old 3DS XL model under the 11.10.0-43E version. Why is this important? Well see Nintendo decided to give one last fuck you, and launched its last update the 11.17.0 which closes off any potential exploits.

Why they decided to do this when they were about to close the eShop is anyone´s guess.

So yeah, if you have a 3DS running on an earlier version, dont update until you mod it unless the instructions explicitly say so. That being said it is still possible to mod in the newest version, but they might need alternative workarounds.

But since I haven´t done it, this makes it much easier since I´m able to do the browser exploit. In this method, I able to temporary unlock the browser despite not being updated and install the hack from there.

## Just Do It

![3DS running on Luma firmware](/images/2023/luma3ds.jpeg#center)

First thing I did was to start transferring the required files to my SD card, and even renaming one of them.

Then per instructions, I changed the proxy server settings then opened up the browser so I could launch the SafeB9SInstaller. It did have an error at first, so I decided to try again, and it worked the second time.

I also installed the boot9strap firmware, did as intructed and rebooted my console. I then managed to transfer one final file to my SD card to finish the setup and check if the firmware was installed properly. So far so good.

It was at this point tht I´m now able to update my 3DS safely, and as a finishing touch I used GodMode9 to do a system backup and transfer those files safely on my PC for safe keeping.

This was done so I could be able to restore my system in case of bricking.

And now I have all the necessary apps right on my console, which includes a homebrew launcher and even an app to install custom themes as well a save manager.

## What Can I Do With This?

![3DS running the homebrew store](/images/2023/update3dsstore.jpeg#center)

A lot of stuff actually. For example, making backups of [physical games I own](https://3ds.hacks.guide/dumping-titles-and-game-cartridges), and install it to my system. Which not only saves me from the effort of having to always switch cartidges, but also in case something happens to my copy.

Another neat feature I found is the homebrew apps. People have gone out of their way to make their own independent games like Minicraft ( a pixel version of Minecraft) or even game ports like Doom (yes it runs Doom).

There´s also utility apps, are you sad that the old youtube app got discontinued, why not get your online video fix with Third Tube? You also got weather apps and even a caculator.

But of course my favorites ones are mods and emulation. For one it can give new life to games I used to play. Animal Crossing Welcome Luxury is great example that provides returning players with quality of life mechanics, there is even a mod for the Mario Kart 7 adding new race tracks.

![a DS gui simulator showing the coraline DS game](/images/2023/twilightmenu.jpeg#center)

Emulation is no slouch either. Well for some reason you can [**actually run DS and GameboyAdvance**](https://wiki.ds-homebrew.com/twilightmenu/) games directly since it shares the same backend software, there is also emulators for the SNES, and even the playstation. Althought, the playstation runs better in a New 3DS.

[**Rverse**](https://www.gamebrew.org/wiki/Rverse_3DS) is an app that allows you to acess the miiverse again, althought I do hear that the **[Pretendo project](https://pretendo.network/)** is planning on offering that feature as well when it finally launches (as well multiplayer servers in case the official ones go down).

Speaking of old features brought back thanks to players, for those who liked the flipnote, there are two online gallery for the [**DSi**](https://www.sudomemo.net/browse/) and the [**3D version**](https://gallery.kaeru.world/) where you can share animations, of course you will need a hacked console to do so.

![3Ds with a loki wallpaper theme](/images/2023/loki3dstheme.jpeg#center)

[**Theme Plaza**](https://themeplaza.art/themes) is for those who aren´t satisfied with just the official themes and want something extra to decorate their gui. As a [**Loki fan**](https://themeplaza.art/item/37511) I picked one that suited to my tastes as I wait for season 2 as of this writing. You even have wacky themes like [**Shrek**](https://themeplaza.art/item/52264) complete with a theme song.

And yes there are NSFW themes in there you can search, so some caution is needed, or not, I´m not your Mom.

To finalize this list, here is the main attraction for why most people will mod their 3DS the **[hShop](https://hshop.erista.me/).** As I mentioned before the eShop is dead and with it its vast library of games.

![the Hshop front store app](/images/2023/hshop.jpeg#center)

This is a solution to that where you can still acess bunch of official software and themes. You can even adquire games that haven´t been released outside certain regions, like a unoficial translated Taiko Drum game.

Is it piracy? Yes, but at this point who cares, the 3DS is officially dead, and in a way this is the only way to preserve these game gems.

There are other things you can do that I haven´t mentioned in this post, but thankfully there is a **[compiled list](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Y-6FGCreVMd7KVyes1rvIroEyJjC9KXQ9vQz72Tq15w/edit)** someone put together that you can see for yourself.

## To Hack or Not To Hack?

The big question here is should anyone do this? Yes.

Do I have any regrets? No! I love having a bit more freedom when it comes to gaming on my console, specially to one as unique and well designed as the 3DS.

Mind you, hacking a console is not without some risk, but as long as you can follow the instructions, the risk of it bricking is almost zero. And you can still play online... well as long as you don´t use cheats. Also, its been a long time since they last made some huge sweep and I don´t think they plan on doing one anytime soon.

So if anyone has an old 3DS sitting out there collecting dust or plan on buying one, go give it a shot.
