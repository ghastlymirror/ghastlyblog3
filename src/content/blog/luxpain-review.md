After managing to mod my 3DS I decide to revisit an old DS favorite of mine.

I remember playing Lux Pain around 2009, but on an emulator, since I didn´t have acess to the console at them time. I think I attempt to finish two time, but the clumsly emulator touch controls made it hard for me to finish.

Still I was fascinated by what I saw after a couple of chapters, finally got to the end years later and now I can say my thoughts on this title.

![the main protagonist saijo a handsome young gray haired man with a tattoed arm](/images/2023/luxpain.jpg#original)

To start off, this game is a psychological horror visual novel game and the plot is about Astsuki Saijo the young agent of an organization called FORT, whose main mission is to hunt and eliminate Silent which are parastic entities that infect people´s minds.

Once infected, the person migh be influenced to commit horrific crimes or suicide, which jsut a heads up, this game does deal with dark themes around mental illness, so keep that in mind.

Now Saijo´s latest mission is to investigate a series of weird events in Kisiragiy City, so he enrolls at the local highschool as an undercover student, and meets a diverse cast of quirky characters along the way.

There´s also a little of highschool drama in there.

Now, one thing to note this is a little different from your typical visual novel. While it does play like a tradional one, there is an active minigame component.

At certain parts of the game I was prompted to use SIGMA, an ability that allows the protagonist to read into people´s minds.

![an example of the scribbling minigame](/images/2023/luxpainscreenshot.jpg#original)

The first two are simple, simply use the touch screen to scribble over a person or location until you find a Worm then press on it until it disappears. Of course if it´s on a person it´s a timed mission, and failing to find them will result in a game over.

But the third minigame is a Boss battle, and it plays like a more hardcore version of whack-a-mole different variations (tip: the second and third variation require you to slash the white dot with your stylus once you unlock it).

After that you receive experience points that will increase your rank, meaning longer times, and higher erasure. These can also be adquired when talking to some people as well.

Of course should you fail its gameover, but you can try again. I also have to mention that clearing or avoiding clearing the silent of certain characters will have a impact on their fate in the plot.

There´s also these little details, that while they don´t have impact on the story progress, they still give a bit of life to the game.

One of my favorite one is visiting the online cafe which allows you to acess online forums, which is steeped in late 2000s random lol culture. I basically get to see how the local comment on the events on the game.

Another location (The Pumpkin Witch), serves to look at the relantionship levels with the other characters in the game.

Outside of these I have acess to a mobile phone and the Fort database. This second is very useful as it allows players to consult information about the case and characters background (unlockable through progress).

And yes a lot of information you get in dialogue can be overwhelming, so you will be using this alot in order to know what is going on.

The mobile phone, only serves to receive messages from the characters once you get their contacts, there is also a news brodacast and you can even change your ring tone. I picked one that was a wacky version of a dog barking a Beethoven song, a gift from Nami.

During the dialogue section part, there are times where there´s a emotional response meter, where certain reactions will impact scenes. Other times you ´ll get a question section where you can simply talk, bring up a specific topic or bring up a memory in order to progress.

![a map of the town of the game](/images/2023/luxpainmap.jpg#medium)

Exploration is done on a grid, some locations have a limited number of turns, in which a route will closed off should pick one of the other. And there´s a couple of option side missions which I often try to do those first before progressing into what I think its the main one.

I usually use the viewing ability to check where each character is in order to plan my choice.

Also just a warning about Chapter 7, because this is one is so easy to screw up and will force you to restart the game completely. At the beginning you will be advised to save before going further ( its also best to have a second save file just in case).

During this phase I was told to find a character in danger. I had a limited number of locations to go through, and the limit was three phone calls. If I don´t find that person before the third call, they die, and its game over.

Its best to just use a walkthrough for this, other than that, this is the only portion of the game that is like this. The rest of the title will just go as normal.

![a blue haired girl with pig tails embracing saijo](/images/2023/luxpaingirl.jpg#medium)


As for the characters, I kinda grew to be fond of them over time, first you have the main student cast: you got sensible artist, an plucky report, a bookworm, a computer nerd, a girl who part times as a fortune teller and of course your typical hothead jock.

Despite initial appearances a lot of these characters are more than they appear and have some tragic backstories to uncover. But there are some funny and heartwarming moments as well as they open up to you.

There´s also some of the teachers, I think my favorite is the excentric teacher with his weird scientific theories, which and is a source for a lot of the comedy.

Outside of the student body there is Nami and her dog Melody, who seems to be able to talk animals and comes to see the protagonist as an older brother figure.

![a nami a young girl and her dog melody](/images/2023/luzpaindog.jpg#original)

The rest of the cast plays a much smaller role and serve to give the town some color, although there were some who I though were just mere side characters who eventully turned into antagonists that I had to confront. It is a game full of twists and turns when you least expect.

Another interesting thing I found out, through some dialogue options the game actually allows players to decide the sexuality of the protagonist (bi, gay or straight), not to mention there is a minor gay character here, in a japanese visual novel outside the bl genre in 2009 no less. he does seem to at least be a positive representation, if you open to him he offers his support should Saijo ever need to talk.

Of course this doesnt impact the game much, as romance isn´t really the focus here, unless you count the out-of-game scenes from extras that you unlock during game progress. But still it is one those small things that I trully apreciate.

Speaking of fun moments, I think I was having a blast during the ghost cat storyline. See there´s this cat who keeps creeping people out and making them upset. And all I could think of was this:

As for the art style it´s decent I suppose with some cute designs. But I really did l love how the game makes you feel like you´re playing throught an anime show. Chapters are called episodes complete with a title card, and [it´s got a cool opening](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6u0pjKZgMbo), sung no other by the same woman who sang for the Evangelion theme.

Seriously the entire sountrack is good, specially during the more sad sections. Its memorable enough that I can still recognize the themes even after a decade.

However, there is one big issue with the game.

![A red jhaired nurse speaking to a blond girl sayng You should come and talk to me more often ](/images/2023/luxpainnurseoffice.jpg#medium)


Its english translation is not very good, dont get me wrong you can still understand the story, but what I mean is there some very clumsy phrases, and one point they keep calling a female character he. Not to mention the game is partially voiced, and what you hear is somehow different than the words on the screen, its like the game got two different scripts.

There´s also the fact the game claims this all takes place in America? This despite the fact that everyone has japanese names, while the foreigners are still adressed as foreign, and they all wear uniforms to school. I guess we´re getting the full 4kids treatment around here.

Other than than that I don´t have many complains.

And before I finish, so far the game only has two endings (making it feel a bit linear), but by playing the game again through an already cleared file you get extra bonuses like starting off with an hight rank, keeping at least 1/4 of experience points and all the characters starting off with a high relantionship level.

There´s also an archive with game art you can unlock.

So if you´re able to get your hands on a physical copy, feel free to give this visual novel a try, or you can go my route have a modded console and go check your rom site of choice.
